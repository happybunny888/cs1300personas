Parking Meter


This parking meter is located in Brownâ€™s Parking lot on Power Street, for users to pay for their parking for a certain amount of time. The Prompt Screen will initially have all the parking rates. The Instruction Figures show users how to use the meter. The users will press on the keypad in response to the prompts shown on the Prompt Screen to select their ticket type, enter the number of hours, and confirm the details. The users can pay by cash or card. Then the ticket will be printed, and users can retrieve it to put on their car dashboard.


Observations

Observations of Users Using the Parking Meter

  • Usually single person per family/car approaches and uses the parking meter.
  • User takes a moment after approaching the parking meter to look at the screen, then they look at the instructions pipeline. A user put their hand up as a cover/shade for the screen.
  • Sometimes there is a delay when comes to the prompting hours screen. Sometimes cancels to retype a number.
  • Users usually pay by card. Then, they grab the ticket, then head back to their car.
  • Usually interactions with the parking meter when purchasing the initial parking ticket is about a minute or less.

Questions for Users

  1. How often do you use the parking meter in Brown's parking lot?
  2. When do you use the parking meter? (During daytime on weekdays, nightime on weekdays, or weekends?)
  3. How long do you park for/how many hours do you pay for?
  4. Do you buy a day pass for parking?
  5. How do you decide how long to park for?
  6. Do you pay by cash or card?
  7. What do you do while your car is parked?
  8. How do you make sure you are back on time?
  9. How often do you add more time to your ticket?
  10. How convenient/easy is it to use this parking meter?
  11. Is there anything you would like to change about the parking meter?
  12. Have you used other parking meters in the city? How do they compare to this meter?

Summary Responses From Users

  • Most users park 3 or 4 hours to go to a Brown University tour and to eat. They do not usually add time to their ticket. One user parked for a day to visit a friend at Brown University.
  • Users often keep an eye on the time, since Brown tours are about 2 hours, and then some time for food and drink, which brings them to almost the ticket's expiry time. One user did say they would set an alarm.
  • Most users will make the final decision at the meter of how long they will park.
  • One user remarked that it is hard to see the text on the screen due to the bright sun. The text changing to yellow when the option is selected is also hard to differentiate.
  • Users say that it is generally easy to use since the screen writes out full instructions and options. This meter accepts card (not only coins) and does not require entering license plate or card info manually. Most have not used the other parking meters in the city, one user remarked that the prices at this meter are more expensive.

Personas

Empathy map for Persona 1: A dad who drove his family from New York, for them to go on a Brown University tour (his son is interested in applying). He is tired from driving for a couple hours and wants some coffee, but is excited to see campus.
Empathy map for Persona 2: A friend who drove on her own from Boston to visit her friend, who is a Brown University student. She is excited to spend the day with her friend.
